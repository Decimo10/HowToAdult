L A U N D R Y

You will need: dirty laundry, laundry detergent (or PODS but not for eating), money (quarters or card) - this stuff isn’t free

Steps:

  1. Sort your laundry. Usually, the rule of thumb is to sort your heavy (dark) and light colors and like color clothes separately. - or if you don’t care, just put everything in the same load.... You should also wait to do laundry when you have a full load and not do half loads.
  2. How much detergent should you use? If you use PODS, just use one per load - DO NOT EAT! If you are using regular liquid, use the top cap as your measuring cup and follow the directions on the bottle. If you are ever in doubt, less is better here - where using less liquid detergent is ok, will still get your clothes clean, and will save you from going through liquid detergent as fast.
  3. Using the washing machine - the dials may be confusing - but they are used to determine how much water should be filled and how long it should wash for.
  4. Make a timer for when to switch your loads (or your wet laundry might be sitting on the gross table when you come find it.
  5. Either air dry or move all your wet laundry to the dryer - it also costs money.

C L E A N I N G   T H E   B A T H R O O M

It is very easy for the bathroom to get disgusting - especially if you are not the only one using it. The first thing to do when cleaning a bathroom is to maintain it - which means a regular schedule for cleaning it. This could be once a week, every other week, etc.

Now to the cleaning part. The three main parts of a bathroom are: the sink, the toilet, the shower. (just in case you didn’t know).

The Sink

If you are having trouble getting your sink to drain properly, pour white vinegar or baking soda down the drain and flush with hot water. For the faucet, you can just use vinegar and a towel to scrub down the faucet and handles.

The Toilet

Toilet - There are a ton of different products that you can buy from the store to clean the toilet. But an easy and cheaper option is to just pour a cup of baking soda in your toilet bowl, scrub, and flush.

TIP: Many of the cleaning products contain very harmful chemicals that are bad for the environment (so another good reason for the baking soda option).

The Shower

The water minerals and soap scum over time will build up in your tub and can be pretty disgusting. A pretty easy hack that you can do is dip a cloth in some white vinegar and scrub your tub. Also, if your shower head starts to get clogged, you can fill a bag full of white vinegar to get the shower head to function better.

S T A I N S

First thing to do when you have just gotten a stain - on clothing or carpet/fabric - Put water on the stain immediately - and do not scrub but . Water works on most things - pretty universal.Detergent, vinegar, lemon juice, salt are other solvents that you can use for specific stains.
